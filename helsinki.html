HELSINKI TEA

This is the refreshing recipe that retains the full flavour of the tea, and has the advantage of being alcohol-free, making it an ideal drink for children who enjoy the taste of tea. Helsinki Tea can be prepared using fruit-flavored teas such as strawberry or raspberry.

Ingrediants

  • 1/2 litre(18 fl oz/ 2 cups)water
  • 4-6 teaspoons tea
  • 50-100g(2-4 oz/ 1/2-1/2 cup) sugar or 4 tablespoons sugar syrup
  • 1 apple
  • 1 untreated lemon
  • 4 fresh pepermint leaves
Method

    Use boiling water to brew the tea for about 4 munites before straining it off and leaving it to cool. To make the sugar syrup, dissolve the sugar in 8 tablespoon boiling water to form a thick syrup. Drop ice cubes into four cups or glasses and add 1 tablespoon of sugar syrup to each. Peel the apple and slice in thinly; place the slice over the ice. Top up with cold tea, and garnish with pepermint leaves and a slice of leamon.

The perfect cup of tea brewed with the world's finest; Ceylon tea is the enchantment that will fill your cup.
